Bias-Variance Tradeoff

We've built and evaluated our loan model. The final question is: what happens when we make the model more complex — more features, higher-degree terms? It fits training data better, but does it generalise? The bias-variance tradeoff is the fundamental tension in all of machine learning.

Three regimes of model complexity

Underfitting (High Bias)
Model is too simple. Misses patterns even in training data. High train error + high test error.
e.g. Always predict "approve" regardless of income.
Just Right
Model captures the true signal without noise. Low train error + low test error. Train and test curves converge near a minimum.
Overfitting (High Variance)
Model memorises training data including noise. Very low train error, high test error.
e.g. Memorise all 8 applicants — fails on new ones.
