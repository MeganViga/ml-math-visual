Confusion Matrix & Metrics

We trained the loan model in Ch 16. Now: how good is it? "Accuracy" alone is misleading — a model that always says "approve" is 50% accurate on our balanced dataset but catches zero defaults. The confusion matrix breaks predictions into four buckets to reveal what really matters.

Performance metrics

–
Accuracy
(TP+TN)/(all)
–
Precision
TP/(TP+FP)
–
Recall
TP/(TP+FN)
–
F1 Score
2·P·R/(P+R)
The recall vs precision tradeoff for loans:
  • Low recall = missing defaults = bank loses money on unpaid loans
  • Low precision = declining good customers = lost revenue & customer complaints
  • The bank must decide which error is more expensive — and set the threshold accordingly
Story so far: The confusion matrix reveals that accuracy alone doesn't tell the whole story. For our loan model, a missed default (FN) costs the bank more than a false alarm (FP).
