Meet the Loan Data

In Ch 1–7 we learned the linear algebra to structure data, make predictions, and solve for θ. Now we go deeper: before fitting a model we need to understand the data. Statistics gives us the tools — distributions, mean, std dev — to see what the loan data is telling us.

Our dataset — 8 past loan applicants. Each row records income, age, loan amount, debt ratio, and whether the person defaulted or repaid. This same dataset runs through every chapter — from matrices (Ch 1) to evaluation (Ch 19).
