Cross-Entropy Loss

In Ch 12, MLE told us to maximise the log-likelihood. Flipping the sign gives us the cross-entropy loss — the number we minimise to train the model. This is the classification equivalent of SSR from Ch 2.

Why we can't use SSR for classification

SSR (regression)

L = Σ(yᵢ − ŷᵢ)² where ŷᵢ ∈ ℝ
Smooth, convex for linear model.
But with sigmoid, SSR is non-convex — gradient descent gets stuck in local minima.

Cross-Entropy (classification)

L = −Σ[yᵢ log(p̂ᵢ) + (1−yᵢ)log(1−p̂ᵢ)]
Always convex with sigmoid.
One global minimum → gradient descent always converges.

Why this matters for the loan model: Cross-entropy loss penalises confident wrong predictions heavily — exactly what we want. If our model says 99% chance the applicant will repay and they default, the loss is enormous. This makes the model more careful, especially for high-stakes errors.
